    • Co-Developing SingaporeWALK (Wearables & Apps in Community Living & Knowledge) Mobile Health App

    Improving Seniors' Physical Health and Mental Well-Being Through Inclusive Technologies

    URECA Undergraduate Research Programme, Research Poster

    Project Overview

    Objective: Co-design an mHealth application, SingaporeWALK, to enhance healthcare delivery for older adults in Singapore.


    Approach: Conducted co-design workshops with 10 older adults, 5 healthcare providers, 5 community center managers, and 5 tech developers to gather insights, ideate features, and test usability.


    Findings: Identified motivational drivers and usability barriers, leading to the proposal of new features and usability enhancements.


    Recommendations: Implement video guides, enhanced readability, goal-oriented rewards, and AI nutrition tracking to improve user engagement and app functionality.


    Impact: Potential to increase mHealth app adoption among older adults, improve health outcomes, and streamline healthcare delivery.

    Problem Statement

    The ageing population in Singapore presents opportunities for mHealth applications to enhance healthcare delivery. However, adoption and engagement levels among older adults remain low. This project aims to bridge this gap by co-designing an mHealth application that caters to the unique lifestyle and health requirements of Singapore's older adults.

    Methodology

    Insights Gathering: Collected prior experiences with mHealth apps, identified motivational drivers, and usability barriers.

    Usability Testing: Conducted structured surveys to quantify user experience using the Mobile App Rating Scale (MARS).

    Results

    Motivational Drivers: Incentives such as household vouchers and communal activities were key engagement drivers.


    Usability Barriers: mHealth apps generally do not support multilingual interfaces and often require complex navigation and data entries.


    Usability Survey Findings: Presentation scored below expectations, with users expressing dissatisfaction with icon and text sizes and color contrast.

    Conclusion

    Initial findings highlight the necessity for mHealth applications to be more user-centric, particularly for older adults. While SingaporeWALK shows promise in engaging this demographic, specific areas for enhancement were identified. Future work involves iterative development with stakeholder inputs to ensure effective adoption and engagement of the app.
